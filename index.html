Succulent Plants

Succulent are growing in popularity as indoor plants. These plants have developed the ability to store water when it is available, for use during dry periods so they should never be over watered. Most of them are winter-hardy, surviving freezing temperatures and snow cover.

Tropical Plants

Because many tropical house plants originally grew in the shady canopy layer of the rainforest, they usually do quite well in indoor settings far from the suns direct rays. Tropical house plants should not be watered so much that they accumulate a pool of water in the outside container.

Fruit Plants

If you keep your subtropical fruit plants and trees outside during the warm months and inside during the winter, you'll need to acclimate your plants to the change in growing environment. Subtropical fruits grown in containers require more frequent watering than those grown in the ground.
